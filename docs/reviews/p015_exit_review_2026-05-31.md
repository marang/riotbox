## What Shipped

P015 turned the existing P012/P013/P014 proof surfaces into more usable Jam
behavior:

- Jam perform and inspect surfaces now expose compact taste/proof language
  without hiding deeper proof detail.
- Recipe 16 gives the musician a short path for reading Jam taste/proof before
  trusting the next move.
- Help overlay copy explains cautious, scene-ready, and proof language inside
  the instrument.
- The screenshot/docs glossary keeps P015 proof vocabulary durable and
  musician-facing.
- First-run next-move guidance and Recipe 16 now respect the same timing-trust
  boundary: `y` scene jump is first-choice only when Jam says
  `taste scene-ready`; cautious, sketch, or unknown taste steers toward
  `g` follow or `f` fill first.
- Help overlay height/readability coverage keeps first-run, taste/proof,
  primary gesture, and advanced sections visible in dense states.

For the software, this reduces drift between proof-heavy internals and the
perform-facing Jam surface. For the musician, Riotbox now gives a clearer answer
to "what changed, can I trust it, and what should I press next?"
